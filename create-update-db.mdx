## The Data Lake

The Data Lake is your central repository for creating, managing, and editing all your datasets. To begin, select an existing database from the dropdown or create a new one.

## Creating a Database

You can add a database by creating one from scratch or by importing a CSV file.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Create from Scratch">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Start Creation">
        Click the **"Create DataBase"** button.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Define and Create">
        In the modal, give your database a unique **Name**, define its initial **Columns**, and click **"Create"**.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Import from CSV">
    <Steps>
      <Step title="Start Import">
        Click the **"Create from File"** button.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Upload Your File">
        Select and upload your CSV file. The system will automatically create the database structure from your file's headers.
      </Step>
    </Steps>
    <Note>
      Your CSV file must include a header row, as these values will be used for the column names.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Editing a Database

Once a database is selected, you can edit its content (rows) and structure (columns).

#### Managing Rows

| To...         | Do this...                                                               |
| :------------ | :----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Add a Row**   | Click the **"Add New Row"** button at the bottom of the table.           |
| **Edit a Row** | Click on the row to edit its content.                               |
| **Delete a Row**| Hover over the row and click the **trash can icon** on the right.        |

#### Managing Columns

To add, rename, or delete columns:

1.  Click the **"Manage Columns"** button.
2.  In the modal that appears, you can:
    *   Rename a column by editing its name.
    *   Delete a column using the **trash can icon**.
    *   Add a new column via **"Add Another Column"**.
3.  Click **"Apply Changes"** to save.

---



#### Exporting to CSV
Select your database and click the **"Download CSV"** button at the bottom of the table to download a full copy.

#### Deleting a Database
Select your database and click the **red trash can icon** next to the "Manage Columns" button. You will be asked to confirm this action.

<Info>
  **Warning:** Deleting a database is a permanent action and cannot be undone.
</Info>
